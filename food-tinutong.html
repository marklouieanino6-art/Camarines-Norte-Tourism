Tinutong

What is Tinutong?

Tinutong is a traditional Bicolano snack or meryenda made by toasting or roasting rice, corn, or other grains over a fire or a pan. The word "tinutong" comes from the Bikol word meaning "toasted" or "roasted."

It is a simple, affordable, and filling snack that has been enjoyed by generations of Bicolano families. It can be eaten plain or paired with local sweets like latik (coconut caramel).

Variants of Tinutong

  • Tinutong na Mais - roasted corn kernels
  • Tinutong na Bigas - toasted rice
  • Tinutong na Mani - roasted peanuts (a common variation)

Where to Buy It

Tinutong is sold in local markets and by street vendors in Camarines Norte. It is a common snack in rural areas and is often sold in small plastic bags at very affordable prices.

Pasalubong Idea: Packaged tinutong makes for a good and inexpensive pasalubong (souvenir) to bring home for family and friends.
