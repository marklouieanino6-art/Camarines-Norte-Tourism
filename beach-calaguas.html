Calaguas Island

About Calaguas

Calaguas is a group of islands located in the municipality of Mercedes, Camarines Norte. It is considered one of the most beautiful and least commercialized island destinations in the Philippines. The main beach, Mahabang Buhangin (Long Beach), features fine white sand and clear turquoise water.

Because there is no electricity or cellular signal in most parts of Calaguas, it is the perfect place to disconnect from technology and enjoy nature.

Things to Do

  • Swimming and snorkeling in clear waters
  • Beach camping (tents and sleeping gear recommended)
  • Island hopping around the Calaguas group
  • Photography of the natural scenery
  • Bonfire nights on the beach

How to Get There

To reach Calaguas, you need to travel to the municipality of Mercedes in Camarines Norte. From Mercedes, you take a motorized boat (bangka) to the island. The boat ride takes about 1.5 to 2 hours depending on sea conditions. It is advisable to arrange your boat ride in advance with a local tour organizer.

What to Bring

  • Enough food and water for your stay
  • Tent or hammock for sleeping
  • Sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Flashlight or battery-powered lantern
  • Power bank for charging devices
  • Cash (no ATMs on the island)
Important: Practice responsible tourism at Calaguas. Bring your trash back with you and do not damage coral reefs. The island's natural beauty depends on how well visitors take care of it.
